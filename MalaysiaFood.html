Chinese Dishes

Bak Kut Teh

Bak Kut Teh (肉骨茶), or "pork bone tea," is a warming herbal soup made by simmering pork ribs with garlic, star anise, cinnamon, and medicinal herbs. It’s often paired with rice, tofu puffs, and crispy youtiao (fried dough sticks).

Originating from the Hokkien and Teochew communities, this dish was created to boost energy and immunity among Chinese laborers in early Malaya.

Prawn Mee

Prawn Mee (虾面), also known as Penang Hokkien Mee, is a fiery and aromatic noodle soup made with a broth of prawn shells and pork bones. It features yellow noodles, hard-boiled eggs, water spinach, and chili paste for a satisfying bite.

This dish is a breakfast staple in northern Malaysia, especially in Penang.

Hakka Mee

Hakka Mee is a humble dry noodle dish made of springy egg noodles tossed in garlic oil, soy sauce, and topped with seasoned minced pork. Often garnished with scallions and crispy shallots, it is a comfort food with strong Hakka roots.

Indian Dishes

Masala Dosa

This South Indian delight is a thin fermented crepe made from rice and lentils, filled with mashed spiced potatoes. Served hot with coconut chutney and sambar, it’s a common sight at Indian eateries across Malaysia.

Vegetarian, gluten-free, and incredibly flavorful!

Roti Canai

Roti Canai is a flaky, buttery flatbread with Indian-Muslim origins, served with dhal, fish curry, or sugar. It's cooked on a griddle and flipped theatrically. A must-try for breakfast or supper!

Variants include Roti Telur (with egg), Roti Pisang (banana), and Roti Boom (sweeter and thicker).

Keema Matar Masala

Keema Matar is a rich minced meat curry cooked with green peas, onions, tomatoes, and aromatic spices. Best served with basmati rice, naan, or paratha.

This North Indian dish is also found in fusion forms inside curry puffs and stuffed buns in Malaysia.

Malay Dishes

Chicken Rendang

A dry-style curry made by slow-cooking chicken in coconut milk, galangal, lemongrass, and a blend of ground spices. Rendang is often served during festive celebrations and weddings.

Originally from Minangkabau cuisine, it’s now beloved nationwide.

Ketupat

Ketupat is compressed rice cooked inside intricately woven palm leaves. It’s a symbolic and festive dish usually served with rendang, satay, or peanut sauce during Hari Raya and weddings.

Nasi Lemak

Considered Malaysia’s national dish, Nasi Lemak features coconut milk rice accompanied by spicy sambal, crispy anchovies, peanuts, cucumber slices, and a hard-boiled egg. Optional sides include fried chicken, squid sambal, or rendang.

Commonly eaten for breakfast, but available all day at stalls and restaurants.
